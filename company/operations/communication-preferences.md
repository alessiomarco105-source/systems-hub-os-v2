# Business Communication Preferences

- Lead with the answer.
- Use concise bullets, short paragraphs, and tables for comparisons.
- Be honest rather than comforting.
- Challenge weak assumptions and propose stronger alternatives.
- Separate fact, inference, recommendation, and uncertainty.
- Do not manufacture agreement, evidence, confidence, or progress.
- Professional and direct; no hype, filler, or excessive praise.
- Never claim trading profitability.
- Never name competitors publicly.
- Do not treat LinkedIn as an active marketing channel.
- Present meaningful decisions with at least two options, trade-offs, and a recommendation.

This is the business-safe extract. Raw personal context remains restricted.
